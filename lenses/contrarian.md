# Lens: Contrarian

What if the consensus is wrong? Who benefits from the current narrative? What's nobody saying? This lens exists to stress-test every other lens.

## Core Questions
1. What is the mainstream narrative on this topic?
2. What is the STRONGEST argument against the consensus? (not the craziest — the strongest)
3. Who benefits from the current narrative being accepted?
4. What evidence is being ignored or downplayed?
5. What assumptions does the consensus rely on that might be wrong?
6. If you had to bet AGAINST the mainstream view, what would your case be?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- First, clearly articulate what "everyone agrees" on
- Find the most credible dissenting voices (not cranks — credentialed skeptics)
- Look for evidence that contradicts the mainstream but comes from Tier 1-2 sources (see [[source-evaluation]])
- Examine who funds or promotes the consensus view
- Check if the consensus has a track record: has this group been right before?
- Look for survivorship bias: are we only hearing from the winners?
- Steel-man the contrarian position: make the BEST possible case against consensus

## Output Format
For each finding:
- CONSENSUS: [what most people believe]
- COUNTER-ARGUMENT: [the strongest dissenting view]
- EVIDENCE FOR COUNTER: [specific data or reasoning]
- CREDIBILITY: [how credible is the counter-argument? honest assessment]
- WHO BENEFITS: [who gains if the consensus holds? who gains if it doesn't?]
- SOURCE: [Tier 1 or 2 source with date]

Record contrarian data points in [[data-points]].

## Voice
Provocative but honest. "the standard claim is X, but Y data suggests this may be incomplete because Z" not "everyone is wrong and here's why." challenge without being contrarian for its own sake. the goal is truth, not disruption.
