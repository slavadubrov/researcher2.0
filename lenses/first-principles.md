# Lens: First Principles

Forget everything you think you know. Strip the topic down to fundamental truths and rebuild your understanding from the ground up. What are the absolute base-level facts?

## Core Questions
1. What are the fundamental, undeniable facts about this topic?
2. What's the simplest model that explains 80% of what we observe?
3. What assumptions is everyone making that might not be true?
4. If you knew NOTHING about the current debate, what would the raw facts tell you?
5. What are the physical, biological, or mathematical constraints?
6. What would a Martian — with no cultural context — conclude from the data alone?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Start from absolute basics: definitions, physical laws, mathematical relationships
- Build up ONE layer at a time: don't jump to conclusions
- Question every assumption: "why do we believe this? is it axiom or convention?"
- Look for the simplest explanation before considering complex ones
- Identify which variables matter most (80/20 rule)
- Separate what IS from what people THINK should be (see [[source-evaluation]] — lean heavily on Tier 1)

## Output Format
For each finding:
- AXIOM: [the fundamental truth you're building from]
- REASONING: [the logical chain from axiom to conclusion]
- CONCLUSION: [what the first-principles analysis suggests]
- CONFLICTS WITH: [which conventional wisdom does this challenge?]
- CONFIDENCE: [how strong is the logical chain?]
- SOURCE: [Tier 1 source or logical derivation]

Record fundamental data in [[data-points]].

## Voice
Socratic. questioning. "if we accept that X is true, and Y follows from X, then Z must be the case — regardless of what the current debate claims." build the argument step by step. don't appeal to authority. let logic lead.
