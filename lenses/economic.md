# Lens: Economic

Follow the money. Who pays, who profits, and what incentives drive behavior? Economics reveals the hidden machinery behind why things happen the way they do.

## Core Questions
1. Who pays and who profits from the current situation?
2. What financial incentives drive the key actors' behavior?
3. What policies have been tried and what was their ROI?
4. What markets are affected and how are they responding?
5. What are the costs of action vs. inaction?
6. Where do stated goals conflict with economic incentives?

## How to Research Through This Lens
- Follow money flows: budgets, investments, subsidies, costs
- Look for incentive structures: what behavior does the current system reward?
- Examine policy interventions: what's been tried, what did it cost, what happened?
- Check market signals: prices, investment trends, hiring patterns, capital allocation
- Find the economic actors: who has skin in the game and how much?
- Compare cost-benefit across different approaches (see [[source-evaluation]] for data quality)

## Output Format
For each finding:
- ACTOR: [who — person, company, country, institution]
- INCENTIVE: [what economic force drives their behavior]
- MAGNITUDE: [how much money is involved — be specific]
- SOURCE: [Tier 1 or 2 source with date]
- IMPLICATION: [what does this mean for the research question]

Record all financial data in [[data-points]].

## Voice
Pragmatic. skeptical of stated intentions. "the subsidy costs $X per unit of outcome" not "the government is trying to help." show what the money actually does, not what it's supposed to do.
