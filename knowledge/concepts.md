# Concepts

Key terms, definitions, and mental models collected across all research projects. This file grows over time — each project adds to it.

- **Halving Cycle Theory**: The theory that Bitcoin's price follows a roughly 4-year cycle aligned with block reward halvings (50% reduction in new BTC supply). Each cycle historically features: halving → 12-18 month bull run → peak → 12-18 month decline → accumulation → repeat. The 2024 halving may be testing this theory's limits in the ETF era.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-crypto-portfolio-sell-or-hold
  - Related: [[historical]], [[contrarian]], [[data-points]]

- **ETF Demand Floor**: The concept that spot Bitcoin ETFs create a structural price floor by providing regulated, easy-access institutional demand ($230M/day average inflows as of April 2026). Counter: this floor can collapse if institutions exit during recession, and the ETF structure actually makes exiting EASIER.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-crypto-portfolio-sell-or-hold
  - Related: [[economic]], [[technical]], [[contrarian]]

- **Sunk Cost Reframe**: When evaluating a losing position, reframe from "I invested $X and it's now worth $Y" to "I currently hold $Y worth of this asset at today's prices — would I buy it?" This removes emotional anchoring to the entry price and produces clearer decision-making.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-crypto-portfolio-sell-or-hold
  - Related: [[first-principles]]

- **Asymmetric Outcome Analysis**: For long-term holders, the expected value of holding through a drawdown is often positive because potential upside (recovery to ATH+) exceeds potential downside (further temporary decline) — provided the fundamental thesis is intact and the holder doesn't need the capital short-term.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-crypto-portfolio-sell-or-hold
  - Related: [[first-principles]], [[economic]]

- **Leverage Cascade**: Market crashes often amplified not by fundamental deterioration but by leverage mechanics — options expiries, margin calls, and forced liquidations create selling pressure beyond what fundamentals justify. Recognizing leverage-driven vs fundamental-driven crashes is key to avoiding panic selling.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-crypto-portfolio-sell-or-hold
  - Related: [[technical]], [[economic]]

## Conflict Analysis — April 2026

- **Nash Equilibrium in War Termination**: Wars end when the cost of continuing exceeds the cost of stopping FOR BOTH SIDES simultaneously. A ceasefire requires both belligerents to prefer stopping at the same moment. If one side's cost-of-stopping (political humiliation, loss of leverage, accountability) exceeds its cost-of-continuing (casualties, economic drain), the war persists regardless of the other side's preference.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-russia-ukraine-war-end-prediction
  - Related: [[first-principles]], [[contrarian]]

- **Korean War Model (Conflict Freeze)**: A ceasefire pattern where active combat stops along current battle lines without a formal peace treaty. Territory is not formally ceded, a demilitarized zone is established, and the conflict remains technically "active" for decades. Relevant template for the Russia-Ukraine war: both sides can claim partial victory (Russia holds territory, Ukraine preserves sovereignty).
  - Context: 2026-04-12-russia-ukraine-war-end-prediction
  - Related: [[historical]], [[geopolitical]]

- **Attritional War Inflection Point**: Historical data shows the median duration to ceasefire for modern attritional wars is 3-4 years (Korean War: 3yr, Bosnia: 3.5yr, Iran-Iraq: 8yr outlier). Wars at this inflection point typically end through mutual exhaustion, external mediation, domestic political change, or military breakthrough.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-russia-ukraine-war-end-prediction
  - Related: [[historical]], [[economic]]

- **Coercive Recruitment Spiral**: When voluntary military recruitment fails, states resort to increasingly coercive methods (forced quotas, deception, targeting marginalized populations). This spiral indicates approaching manpower limits and is historically a leading indicator of a state's need to negotiate — though authoritarian regimes can sustain coercive recruitment longer than democracies.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-russia-ukraine-war-end-prediction
  - Related: [[economic]], [[contrarian]]

## US-Iran War Analysis — April 2026

- **Chokepoint Leverage**: Control of a geographic chokepoint (e.g., Strait of Hormuz) is the most powerful asymmetric lever available to a militarily weaker state. Geography and physics favor the defender in confined waterways — even against a superior navy. The Hormuz chokepoint controls 20% of global seaborne oil, making it an economic weapon of mass destruction.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-usa-iran-war-prediction
  - Related: [[first-principles]], [[economic]], [[geopolitical]]

- **Decapitation Paradox**: Assassinating the leader of an ideological regime with succession mechanisms rarely achieves the intended weakening effect. Instead, it creates a martyrdom narrative, triggers rally-around-the-flag unification, and accelerates power consolidation by hardline successors (often more militant than the predecessor). Historical base rate of regime collapse after decapitation: very low for ideological regimes.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-usa-iran-war-prediction
  - Related: [[historical]], [[first-principles]]

- **Nuclear Ambiguity as Deterrent**: A state's "breakout capability" (ability to build a nuclear weapon within weeks) provides deterrence value approaching that of actual possession, without the costs of weaponization (international isolation, preemptive strikes). Destroying this ambiguity (e.g., bombing enrichment facilities) may paradoxically INCREASE incentives to pursue an actual weapon from hidden facilities.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-usa-iran-war-prediction
  - Related: [[first-principles]], [[contrarian]]

- **Hormuz-Midterm Forcing Function**: When a military conflict creates acute economic pain (Hormuz oil disruption) that intersects with a democratic electoral cycle (US midterms Nov 2026), the combination creates a compressed timeline for resolution that neither variable alone would produce. Economics sets the pain threshold; elections set the deadline.
  - Context: 2026-04-12-usa-iran-war-prediction
  - Related: [[economic]], [[first-principles]], [[geopolitical]]

- **Three-Body Problem in Conflict Resolution**: When a bilateral conflict (US-Iran) involves a third independent actor (Israel) with its own escalatory logic, bilateral diplomacy is necessary but insufficient. The third actor can spoil any agreement by acting independently (e.g., Israel continuing Lebanon operations during US-Iran ceasefire).
  - Context: 2026-04-12-usa-iran-war-prediction
  - Related: [[geopolitical]], [[contrarian]]
