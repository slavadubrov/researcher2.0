# Output Evaluation

Quality criteria for the 4 research deliverables. Use this to evaluate your outputs BEFORE finalizing them. If an output doesn't meet the minimum bar, revise it.

> **Lineage:** This adapts the intelligence community's production standards — every finished intelligence product must meet defined quality gates before dissemination. Also draws on peer review criteria from academic publishing and Tetlock's calibration methodology. Key lesson from IC production: analysts who self-evaluate against explicit rubrics produce measurably better work than those who rely on instinct alone.

## Executive Summary Rubric

See [[executive-summary-template]] for structure.

### Must Include
- Bottom-line assessment (2-3 sentences, not a paragraph of hedging)
- Confidence level for each key finding (high / medium / low)
- At least one unresolved tension between lenses
- Top open questions (link to [[open-questions]])
- What evidence would change the conclusion

### Must NOT
- Exceed 500 words
- Present single-lens findings as conclusions
- Resolve all tensions into false agreement
- Omit confidence levels
- Read like it could have been written without the lens analysis

### Minimum Bar
Could someone make a decision based on this? If not, it's a summary, not an executive summary.

## Deep Dive Rubric

See [[deep-dive-template]] for structure.

### Must Include
- Findings organized by lens with cross-references between them
- Contradictions explicitly flagged (not buried)
- Source tiers noted for key claims
- Second-order insights from lens combinations
- Methodology notes (what was searched, what was unavailable)

### Must NOT
- Present lenses as independent silos (cross-reference is required)
- Hide disagreements in footnotes or asides
- Rely on a single tier for any major claim
- Skip any core lens designated by [[research-frameworks]]

### Minimum Bar
Does the deep dive contain insights that ONLY emerge from multi-lens analysis? If every section could stand alone without referencing other lenses, the synthesis failed.

## Key Players Rubric

See [[key-players-template]] for structure.

### Must Include
- Actors from at least 3 different lenses
- Both stated positions AND actual incentives
- Influence level assessment
- Relationships between actors where relevant

### Must NOT
- List only the obvious actors (the value is in who gets MISSED)
- Conflate what actors SAY with what they DO
- Omit the lens that surfaced each actor

### Minimum Bar
Would this list surprise someone who only read mainstream coverage? If not, dig deeper.

## Open Questions Rubric

See [[open-questions-template]] for structure.

### Must Include
- Questions that are specific and researchable (not rhetorical)
- What evidence would answer each question
- Why each question matters for the research goal
- Which lens surfaced the question

### Must NOT
- Include questions already answered by the research
- Be vague ("What about the economy?" is not a question)
- Omit the "what evidence would answer this" field

### Minimum Bar
Could each open question become a future research project? If not, it's too vague or already answered.

## Comparison Test

After completing all outputs, ask:
1. Does this analysis contain insights that a single well-crafted prompt to an AI would NOT produce?
2. Are there genuine tensions preserved (not everything neatly resolved)?
3. Did any lens change your mind about something?
4. Would you trust this analysis enough to act on it?

If the answer to any of these is "no," identify which step in the process broke down and revise.
